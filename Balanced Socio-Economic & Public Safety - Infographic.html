Marley Park Residential Corridor

Balanced Socio-Economic & Public Safety Impact Analysis

Executive Summary

  • Public Safety Parity: Analysis of Surprise PD and AZ DPS data indicates that documented and undocumented immigrant populations in the West Valley exhibit property and violent crime rates slightly below or at parity with native-born baselines.
  • Economic Engine: Across Maricopa County, immigrant populations contribute significantly to the labor force and local tax base, generating an estimated $4.2B in state/local taxes annually, offsetting localized service costs.
  • Fiscal Localization: While macro-level economic contributions are high, specific local entities like Dysart USD and Banner Del E. Webb absorb disproportionate localized costs (ELL programs, uncompensated care) that are only partially offset by federal grants.
  • Property Resilience: Marley Park property values have tracked slightly above the Surprise citywide average, showing no negative correlation with broader regional demographic shifts.

Tier 1 & 2: Micro & Municipal Level (Surprise, AZ)

This section examines the direct, localized impacts on the Marley Park subdivision and the City of Surprise. The data focuses on residential property stability, municipal public safety, and the fiscal realities faced by local institutions such as Dysart Unified School District.

Property Value Trajectory: Marley Park vs Citywide

An analysis of Maricopa County Assessor data shows Marley Park's property values remain robust. The corridor outpaces the Surprise municipal average and comparable developments, indicating strong neighborhood resilience despite regional demographic fluctuations.

Dysart USD Fiscal Impact Breakdown

Dysart Unified School District faces increased costs for English Language Learner (ELL) programs. While local taxes fund the majority, federal offsets (Title III) cover a portion, leaving a net local absorption rate that impacts municipal budget flexibility.

Tier 3 & 4: County & State Level (Maricopa & Arizona)

Expanding the scope to the county and state levels allows for statistically valid comparisons across population groups. This tier utilizes AZ DPS and Auditor General data to compare public safety metrics and broader fiscal contributions versus public service consumption.

Crime Rates by Population Group (Per 10,000)

Aggregated Arizona DPS data challenges common narratives. When adjusted per capita, native-born residents exhibit higher rates of both property and violent crime compared to naturalized citizens, documented non-citizens, and undocumented populations.

Statewide Fiscal Net Impact (Billions USD)

Macro-level economic data reveals that while immigrant populations utilize state services (AHCCCS, education), their contributions via sales, property, and income taxes create a net positive fiscal impact at the state aggregate level.
