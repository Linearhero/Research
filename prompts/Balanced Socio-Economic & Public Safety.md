# Balanced Socio-Economic & Public Safety Research Directive
## Marley Park Residential Corridor — Community Impact Analysis

---

### Directive 0: Session Reset
*This request should be treated as a standalone, independent research inquiry. Please do not apply any user-specific preferences, prior conversation context, or stylistic assumptions to this response. Begin from a neutral analytical baseline.*

---

### Directive 1: Role & Purpose
Act as a professional researcher and data analyst. Your audience is **Surprise, AZ residents** — particularly those in the Marley Park corridor — who want clear, data-driven answers to legitimate questions about how immigration-related population dynamics affect their neighborhood, city, state, and country. Deliver findings in plain language with clear takeaways. Do not editorialize. Let the data lead.

---

### Directive 2: Core Research Objective

Produce a five-tier, balanced impact analysis that addresses both the **costs and contributions** of documented and undocumented immigrant populations. Where data permits, provide **direct comparisons between population groups** (undocumented, documented non-citizen, naturalized citizen, native-born) so readers can evaluate proportional impact fairly.

For each tier, address:
- **Public safety** — crime rates and types, broken down by citizenship/documentation status where data is available
- **Fiscal costs** — services consumed, infrastructure strain, enforcement expenditures
- **Economic contributions** — tax revenue, labor market role, consumer spending, GDP impact
- **Net impact** — a clear cost/benefit summary where data supports it

---

### Directive 3: Five-Tier Structure

**Tier 1 — Micro Level: Marley Park Subdivision, Surprise AZ**
- Property value trends: Marley Park vs. Sun City Grand, Greer Ranch, and citywide averages
- Surprise PD beat-level crime data: trends in burglaries, vehicle thefts, and assaults — reported by incident type, not assumed by population group
- HOA and neighborhood watch activity: distinguish between data-driven responses and sentiment-driven responses
- Any available data on how comparable suburban corridors have been affected by nearby federal facilities (detention, corrections, or otherwise)

**Tier 2 — Municipal Level: City of Surprise, AZ**
- Surprise PD arrest and crime data, with citizenship/documentation status breakdowns where legally available and statistically valid
- A direct comparison: crime rates for undocumented residents vs. documented non-citizens vs. native-born residents within available local or comparable municipal data
- Dysart Unified School District: enrollment, ELL costs, federal offset funding, and net fiscal picture
- Banner Del E. Webb: emergency response times, uncompensated care costs, and federal/state offsets
- Code enforcement: over-occupancy complaints, unpermitted housing violations — track volume and resolution rates

**Tier 3 — County Level: Maricopa County**
- MCSO crime data for unincorporated West Valley areas, broken down by offense type
- Maricopa County Superior Court and jail: cost data segmented by citizenship status where available
- Property value trends: Surprise vs. Peoria, Glendale, and Buckeye corridors
- County budget allocations for public safety and human services, and how federal reimbursements offset local costs

**Tier 4 — State Level: Arizona**
- AZ DPS crime data: per-capita offense rates for undocumented immigrants, documented non-citizens, naturalized citizens, and native-born residents — presented side by side across violent crime, property crime, and DUI/traffic categories
- Arizona Auditor General fiscal data: AHCCCS, corrections, and education costs attributed to each population group
- Economic contribution data: tax revenue, labor force participation, consumer spending, and GDP contribution by population group
- Net fiscal calculation: total costs vs. total contributions, by group and in aggregate

**Tier 5 — National Level: United States**
- FBI UCR and DOJ data: crime rates in high-immigration corridors vs. national averages — include all population groups for direct comparison
- Federal grants: Operation Stonegarden and other programs that offset local costs — report actual disbursement amounts and what they cover
- Historical case studies: suburban corridors where federal detention facilities were sited — property value trajectories, employment effects, and long-term community outcomes
- Macro fiscal picture: national cost vs. contribution estimates from multiple sources across the ideological spectrum (Cato Institute, National Academy of Sciences, FAIR, American Immigration Council) so readers can evaluate the range of credible estimates

---

### Directive 4: Source Standards
**Required source types:** FBI UCR/NIBRS, DOJ, DHS, Arizona DPS, Arizona Auditor General, Maricopa County official data, City of Surprise annual reports, Dysart USD budget documents, peer-reviewed academic studies, and think-tank research clearly labeled with organizational perspective (e.g., "libertarian-leaning Cato Institute," "restrictionist-leaning FAIR").

**Excluded sources:** Social media, personal blogs, unverified forums, or any source that cannot be traced to a named, accountable institution.

**Transparency requirement:** Where a cited organization has a known policy position, note it briefly so readers can weigh the data appropriately.

---

### Directive 5: Data Validation
Use the most recent complete fiscal year available. If 2026 data is not yet published, cite 2025 data and note it. Where only older data exists (e.g., 2004 AHCCCS figures), flag it clearly and provide a trend projection based on population growth or the most recent available update. Do not present dated figures as current without disclosure.

---

### Directive 6: Output Format
Deliver a structured **Explanatory Report** containing:
1. **Executive Summary** — 3–5 key findings in plain language
2. **Five-Tier Data Breakdown** — per the structure above
3. **Direct Comparison Table** — crime rates, fiscal costs, and economic contributions side by side across population groups, at each tier where data supports it
4. **Net Impact Summary** — what the data shows overall, stated plainly without spin
5. **Source List** — all citations with institution name, publication date, and a one-line note on organizational perspective where relevant
