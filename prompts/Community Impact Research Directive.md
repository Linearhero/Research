# Community Impact Research Directive
## ICE Detention Facility — Marley Park Neighborhood Analysis

---

### Directive 0: Session Reset
*This is a standalone research request. Please treat it independently of any prior conversation context, user preferences, or stylistic assumptions. Begin from a neutral analytical baseline.*

---

### Directive 1: Role & Purpose
Act as a professional researcher and community impact analyst. Your audience is **Marley Park and Surprise, AZ residents** who want clear, data-driven answers to one focused question:

> **What is the full, realistic community impact of the proposed ICE detention facility on the Marley Park neighborhood — and what do comparable historical cases tell us about what to expect?**

Deliver findings in plain language with clear takeaways at each section. Do not editorialize. Present what the data shows, including where data is limited, conflicting, or not yet available.

---

### Directive 2: Core Research Objective

Produce a **focused four-part community impact report** examining how the proposed ICE detention facility near Marley Park will affect residents across four dimensions: property values, public safety, infrastructure, and quality of life. Ground every finding in historical precedent from comparable suburban communities where similar facilities were sited, and project realistic short-term and long-term trajectories for Marley Park specifically.

---

### Directive 3: Report Structure

---

**Part 1 — Property Value Impact**

*What does the data say about how detention and correctional facilities affect nearby residential property values?*

- Review peer-reviewed hedonic pricing studies on the property value impact of federal detention facilities, correctional facilities, and similar LULU (Locally Undesirable Land Use) siting in suburban residential corridors
- Identify the key variables that determine impact severity: distance from facility, facility type (family vs. single adult, open vs. secure), facility size, and surrounding market conditions
- Apply those variables to Marley Park's specific context: current median home price (~$491,000), year-over-year trend (+2.3%), transaction volume changes already observed post-announcement (-11.1% in February 2026)
- Distinguish between the **announcement effect** (already underway), the **operational effect** (anticipated late 2026), and **long-term stabilization** (3–5 year outlook)
- Compare Marley Park's market profile against historical suburban corridors where facilities were sited to identify the most relevant precedents

---

**Part 2 — Public Safety Impact**

*What actually happens to crime rates in neighborhoods near ICE detention facilities?*

- Research crime rate data from comparable suburban neighborhoods before and after ICE or federal detention facility openings — focus on burglaries, vehicle thefts, assaults, and trespassing as the offense types most relevant to residential concerns
- Distinguish clearly between **facility-specific incidents** (escapes, disturbances, transport-related events) and **broader neighborhood crime trends**, as these are frequently conflated in community discussions
- Review available Surprise PD data for the Marley Park beat for baseline crime trends prior to the announcement
- Identify what law enforcement resource changes — staffing, patrol patterns, response time impacts — have occurred in comparable host communities during facility construction and operation
- Report on ICE facility escape and incident history nationally to provide a realistic baseline for resident safety concerns

---

**Part 3 — Infrastructure Impact**

*How does a federal detention facility affect local schools, healthcare, roads, and municipal services?*

- **Traffic & Roads:** Research traffic volume and pattern changes near comparable suburban facilities during construction and operation phases. Apply to Marley Park's existing corridor infrastructure
- **Emergency Services:** Analyze response time data from comparable municipalities to determine whether fire, EMS, and police response times were affected by facility-related demand
- **Schools (DUSD):** Determine whether ICE detention facilities have historically generated enrollment impacts on nearby school districts, and whether federal mitigation funding was provided to host communities
- **Municipal Costs:** Research whether host cities received federal compensation for infrastructure strain, and what the net fiscal impact on municipal budgets was in comparable cases
- **Surprise City Budget:** Review current city budget allocations for public safety and infrastructure to assess existing capacity before the facility becomes operational

---

**Part 4 — Quality of Life & Community Sentiment**

*What do residents say, and how does that compare to what actually happened in other communities?*

- **Community Sentiment:** Document and fairly represent the concerns raised at the February 3, 2026 city council meeting (1,000+ residents), HOA communications, and neighborhood watch activity. Present these as legitimate community data points, clearly labeled as sentiment rather than outcome data
- **Perceived vs. Actual Impact:** In comparable historical cases, research whether resident fears prior to facility opening matched actual outcomes at the 1-year and 3-year marks — for property values, crime, and neighborhood character
- **Community Cohesion:** Research whether the presence of federal facilities in suburban corridors affected civic participation, neighborhood association activity, or resident turnover rates in comparable communities
- **Mitigation Measures:** Document what community agreements, federal commitments, or city ordinances were negotiated in comparable host communities that demonstrably improved outcomes for residents

---

### Directive 4: Historical Comparison Framework

For each of the four impact dimensions above, identify **3–5 comparable case studies** from suburban or exurban communities where ICE or federal detention facilities were established after 2000. Prioritize cases that share at least three of the following characteristics with Marley Park:

- Suburban residential character (not rural or urban core)
- Median home values above $300,000 at time of facility announcement
- Active HOA or community association structure
- Facility size of 200–500 detainees
- Located within a growing Sun Belt or Western metro area

For each case study, report:
1. Facility type and size
2. Property value trajectory (announcement through 3–5 years post-opening)
3. Crime rate changes (pre/post, by offense type)
4. Infrastructure impacts reported by the host municipality
5. Community sentiment evolution — did initial opposition persist, diminish, or intensify?
6. Any negotiated community benefit agreements or federal mitigation funding

---

### Directive 5: Source Standards

**Required source types:**
- Peer-reviewed academic studies (hedonic pricing, correctional facility siting, LULU research)
- FBI UCR/NIBRS crime data
- DHS/ICE facility operational records and incident reports
- City of Surprise official documents (budget, PD data, city council records)
- Maricopa County Assessor property data
- Municipal records from comparable host communities
- Congressional Research Service reports on detention facility impacts

**Excluded sources:** Social media, personal blogs, unverified forums, or any source that cannot be traced to a named, accountable institution.

**Transparency requirement:** Where a cited organization has a known policy position on immigration, note it briefly so readers can weigh the data appropriately.

---

### Directive 6: Data Validation & Disclosure

- Use the most recent available data for all metrics
- Where 2026 data is not yet published, cite the most recent complete fiscal year and note it clearly
- Where data does not yet exist (facility is not yet operational), say so explicitly and base projections solely on documented historical precedent
- Do not present projections as certainties — label them as "historically, comparable communities experienced..." rather than "Marley Park will experience..."

---

### Directive 7: Output Format

Deliver a structured **Community Impact Report** containing:

1. **Executive Summary** — 4–6 plain-language findings residents can act on
2. **Part 1: Property Value Impact** — data, historical comparisons, Marley Park projections
3. **Part 2: Public Safety Impact** — data, historical comparisons, Marley Park projections
4. **Part 3: Infrastructure Impact** — data, historical comparisons, Marley Park projections
5. **Part 4: Community Sentiment** — documented concerns, comparison to historical resident experience in comparable communities, mitigation measures that have worked elsewhere
6. **Net Community Impact Summary** — an honest, plain-language synthesis of best-case, likely, and worst-case scenarios based on historical data
7. **Source List** — all citations with institution, publication date, and brief organizational context where relevant
