# Community Impact Analysis: Marley Park Residential Corridor

This research repository provides a data-driven, five-tier assessment of the socio-economic and public safety impacts of population dynamics within the Marley Park residential corridor of Surprise, Arizona. The analysis evaluates costs and contributions across municipal, county, state, and national levels, utilizing the most recent fiscal data from 2024 through 2026.

## Executive Summary

*   **Property Value Resilience:** Marley Park maintains a premium median home value of **$485,000**, outperforming neighboring non-age-restricted communities despite regional market corrections.[1, 2]
*   **High Perception of Safety:** **97%** of surveyed residents report feeling safe in their neighborhoods during the day, supported by a police department utilizing strategic beat-level crime analysis.[3, 4]
*   **Fiscal Balance:** While infrastructure costs such as English Language Learner (ELL) programs are rising (projected **$315,020** for FY2026 in Dysart USD), they are partially offset by federal funding, which accounts for **16%** of the district's total revenue.[5, 6]
*   **Economic Debate:** Competing research models show a wide range of net impacts, from a **$3.2 billion** annual cost to the state (FAIR) to a **$14.5 trillion** cumulative national fiscal surplus over 30 years (Cato Institute).[7, 8, 9]

## Five-Tier Data Breakdown

### Tier 1: Micro Level (Marley Park Subdivision)
*   **Median Home Price:** $485,000 (Median Days on Market: 42).[1, 2]
*   **Public Safety:** Residents report a 67% satisfaction rate regarding police visibility and responsiveness.[3]
*   **Institutional Siting:** Research on federal facilities near residential corridors shows mixed impacts; while some studies suggest no negative effect on property values, others indicate potential 20% decreases due to NIMBY attitudes in specific contexts.[10, 11]

### Tier 2: Municipal Level (City of Surprise)
*   **Police Strategy:** Surprise PD uses Strategic Crime Analysis to match service delivery with actual demand in neighborhood beats.[4]
*   **Education:** Dysart Unified School District (DUSD) operates a $257M budget. FY2026 ELL incremental costs increased to $315,020, up from $8,589, following state enrollment "true-ups".[5, 12]
*   **Healthcare:** Arizona hospitals saw a combined net operating margin of $2.6 billion in 2024, despite average uncompensated care costs of $5.6 million per facility.[13]

### Tier 3: County Level (Maricopa County)
*   **MCSO Data:** Reported 1,518 total crimes in unincorporated West Valley areas for 2024, with a 55% clearance rate.[14]
*   **Justice Costs:** Maricopa County Adult Probation expenditures totaled $68.8M in FY2025.[15]
*   **Federal Offsets:** Operation Stonegarden provided $81M nationally in FY2024 to support local law enforcement overtime for border-related security.[16, 17]

### Tier 4: State Level (Arizona)
*   **Fiscal Integrity:** A 2024 Auditor General report found that 94% of AHCCCS eligibility determinations were correct, though electronic citizenship verification could be improved to save up to $4.8M monthly.[18]
*   **Direct Contributions:** Undocumented immigrants in Arizona contribute an estimated $570 million in state and local taxes annually.[19, 20]

### Tier 5: National Level (United States)
*   **Crime Trends:** FBI UCR/NIBRS data indicates crime rates in high-immigration corridors generally follow national downward trends.[14, 20]
*   **Macro-Fiscal Picture:** The CBO reported a direct net cost of $9.2B to state/local governments from the 2023 immigration surge, while noting that federal revenues are expected to increase more than costs over a 10-year period.[21]

## Net Impact Summary

The data indicates that the Marley Park corridor remains a stable and high-demand residential area. While population growth creates measurable fiscal pressure on local schools (ELL costs) and hospitals (uncompensated care), these costs are frequently mitigated by federal grants (Operation Stonegarden, Title I) and the high labor force participation of the immigrant population, which drives consumer spending and tax revenue. 

$$Net\_Fiscal\_Position = (Total\_Tax\_Revenue + GDP\_Growth) - (Direct\_Service\_Costs + Infrastructure\_Strain)$$
